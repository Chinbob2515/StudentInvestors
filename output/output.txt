   <h1 style="text-align:center"><img src="http://si-static1.ifsstudentinvestor.org/images/companies.png" alt="" width="45" height="64"> Company List</h1>

<p>It's not time to play the Student Investor Challenge yet, but you can try out trading stock in our practice game until 14 October. Anything you buy now will be removed from your portfolio once the competition starts on 17 October. This means that you can buy and sell now safe in the knowledge that if you make a bad decision it will not affect your standing in the full competition.</p>
<p style="margin-top:1em">To find out more about each company, or to buy stock in it, just follow the link from its name.</p>

<p style="margin-top:1em">As well as examining the companies available you should <a href="/etf-list.php">see which ETFs you can trade</a> and keep an eye on <a href="/portfolio.php">your team's Practice Game portfolio</a>. You can also pick up some tips on trading by reading the <a href="/portfolio-challenge-how-to.php">Guru's guide on how to pick your portfolio</a> and <a href="/video-guides.php">watching our video guides</a>.</p>


    </div>
 
<div class="postballoon" style="margin-top:32px">
<p>Company stocks are a way of investing in a company by buying small parts of that company, called 'shares'. The value of these shares changes over time depending as to how well investors believe the company is performing. Some companies also hand out a share of their profits to shareholders each year &ndash; this is called a dividend.</p>
<p>Our company stocks are split in to two sections:</p> 
<ul>
<li>First we have companies from <strong>FTSE 100</strong>. These are the '100 most highly capitalised' (i.e. 'biggest') companies that are quoted on the London Stock Exchange, accounting for around 81% of the UK market.
